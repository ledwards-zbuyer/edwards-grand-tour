Sat Jul 18 · The Boat Day · snorkel stop

Christ of the Abyss

In the next bay over, off the tiny abbey cove of San Fruttuoso, a bronze Christ stands on the seafloor with both arms raised toward the surface — placed there in 1954 and now one of the most famous dive sites on Earth. We anchor above it.

56 ftto the base
~33 ftto his hands
8.2 fttall · bronze
  • Visible from the surface on a clear day with just a mask — Zoey and Barbara can see him from the boat while the rest of us free-dive down.
  • The bronze was cast from melted-down ship bells, submarine propellers, and medals donated by sailors, athletes, and soldiers' mothers.
  • He's a memorial to Dario Gonzatti, Italy's first scuba diver, who died near this spot — and a guardian for everyone still in the water.
  • The reserve around him teems with groupers, moray eels, red coral — protected waters since 1999.
  • Bonus stop: the 10th-century San Fruttuoso abbey on the beach behind — reachable only by boat or foot trail. Focaccia on the sand.

Open-time ideas

for the full day
10-min walk · free-ish

The Pantheon

1,900 years old and still the largest unreinforced concrete dome ever built. The roof has a 30-ft open hole — when it rains, it rains inside, and hidden floor drains carry it away. Rome's best 30 minutes.

Go at 7 AM or 10 PM

Trevi Fountain

Coin over the left shoulder with the right hand = you'll return to Rome. The fountain collects ~€3,000 a day, all donated to charity. Zoey gets the first throw.

If the crew has legs

The Colosseum

50,000 seats, emptied in minutes through 80 numbered gates — stadium design we still copy. Book a skip-the-line slot for early morning or accept it from the outside; both are legit with a 3-year-old.

Right outside the door

Campo de' Fiori morning market

Two blocks away: Rome's oldest open market for fruit, flowers, and people-watching. Grab breakfast here, and Frigidarium — one of Rome's best gelaterie — is a 5-minute walk for the after-dinner cone.
